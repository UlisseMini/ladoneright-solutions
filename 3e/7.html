Suppose v,xv,xv,x are vectors in VVV and U,WU,WU,W are subspaces of VVV such that v+U=x+Wv+U=x+Wv+U=x+W. Prove that U=WU = WU=W.

We add −x-x−x to both sides to get (v−x)+U=W(v-x) + U = W(v−x)+U=W which, since (v−x)+U(v-x)+U(v−x)+U is a subspace it must contain zero implying (x−v)∈U(x-v) \in U(x−v)∈U and (since UUU contains inverses) (v−x)∈U(v-x) \in U(v−x)∈U finally giving (v−x)+U=U=W(v-x)+U=U=W(v−x)+U=U=W.

If you're uncomfortable with adding −x-x−x to both sides feel free to rewrite it in terms of components.
